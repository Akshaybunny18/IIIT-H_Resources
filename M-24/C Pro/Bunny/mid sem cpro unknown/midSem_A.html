Problem 1: Dreamoon and Stairs

Dreamoon wants to climb up a stair of n steps. He can climb 1 or 2 steps at each move. Dreamoon wants the number of moves to be a multiple of an integer m.

What is the minimal number of moves making him climb to the top of the stairs that satisfies his condition?

Input:

  • The single line contains two space-separated integers n and m.

Output:

  • Print a single integer — the minimal number of moves being a multiple of m. If no such way exists, print -1.

Constraints:

  • 0 < n ≤ 10000
  • 1 < m ≤ 10

Example:

Input 1:
10 2
Output 1:
6
Input 2:
3 5
Output 2:
-1

Your task is to help him sort this jar so that all the red candies (0s) come first, followed by the green ones (1s), and finally, the blue ones (2s). Think of it as arranging your favorite candies in a perfect rainbow order.

Problem 3:Unlocking the Vault of Xorland: The Power of XOR Queries

In a distant kingdom, there lived a group of scholars tasked with guarding a treasure vault filled with rare artifacts. The king, fascinated by patterns and mysteries, created a secure vault system that was accessible only by solving intricate puzzles. Each artifact in the vault was associated with a unique power level, and these powers were stored in the form of a magical sequence of numbers. The key to the vault could only be uncovered by performing a special operation on parts of this sequence.

The operation required was an XOR computation on sub-sections of the magical sequence, which the scholars referred to as "The XOR Path of Secrets." To further complicate matters, the scholars had to answer a set of challenges—queries given by the king—to unlock different sections of the vault. Each challenge asked them to compute the XOR of a specific range of artifacts' powers, denoted by indices within the magical sequence.

You, as the most brilliant scholar of the realm, have been assigned this task. The king gives you a sequence of power levels representing the artifacts, and for each query, you are to compute the XOR of the subarray from index l to r.

Unlock the vault and reveal the hidden treasures by answering all the king's queries!

Input:

  • The first line contains an integer n, the size of the array.
  • The second line contains n integers, representing the power levels in the array.
  • The third line contains an integer q, the number of queries.
  • The next q lines contain two integers l and r for each query, representing the 1-based indices of the subarray for which the XOR must be computed.

Output:

  • For each query, output a single integer, the XOR of the subarray from index l to r.

Constraints:

  • 1 ≤ n ≤ 2*10^5
  • 1 ≤ q ≤ 2*10^5
  • 0 ≤ a[i] ≤ 1e9
  • 1≤l≤r≤n

Example:

Input 1:
5
3 8 2 6 4
3
1 3
2 5
1 5
Output 1:
9
8
11

  • For the first query (1, 3): XOR of elements from index 1 to 3 → 3 ^ 8 ^ 2 = 9
  • For the second query (2, 5): XOR of elements from index 2 to 5 → 8 ^ 2 ^ 6 ^ 4 = 8
  • For the third query (1, 5): XOR of elements from index 1 to 5 → 3 ^ 8 ^ 2 ^ 6 ^ 4 = 11
